How to successfully grow indoor plants isn’t always easy, but it can be easier if you start with the right mindset. Although it may seem like growing them comes naturally to some people, the truth is that anyone can do it! When you keep these eight things in mind, you’ll be well on your way to growing healthy and beautiful indoor plants year-round.

What are you looking for from your indoor plants?

One thing you’ll have to do is determine what you want from your indoor plants. Do you want them just for aesthetic reasons? Do you want them for air quality? Do you need a natural light source during the winter months, or are your windows adequate? These are all things to consider in growing indoor plants. They’re also things that can help direct your choice of plant species, as some species thrive under different conditions. Choose a few traits that are important and make sure those conditions exist inside your home before choosing which types of plants will live there with you.

How big is your home?

Bigger is better when it comes to indoor plants, especially if you live in a place that gets less than six hours of sunlight per day. Many indoor plants are tropical and will not survive in smaller homes or apartments with limited windows. Generally speaking, if you’re growing an understory plant-like fiddle leaf fig or rubber tree, you want at least 1,000 square feet of living space; larger plants like banyan trees or amazon giants may require more than 2,000 square feet. If you have any doubt about your lighting situation (or just love houseplants), consider talking with a professional landscaper before buying new flora.

A lot of people make mistakes by keeping their plants too close to a window, which can damage their leaves. The best thing for both you and your plants is placing them about two feet away from an eastern or western facing window, depending on whether you live in the northern or southern hemisphere. This way they’ll benefit from natural sunlight during mornings and evenings while still getting some direct sunlight throughout other parts of the day. Don’t worry if you don’t have any windows on those sides of your home; there are lots of other ways to get your houseplants what they need.

Does your home have high humidity?

If you live in a humid climate, it might be difficult for your indoor plants to thrive. When your air is naturally full of moisture, it can be challenging for a plant’s leaves to retain water and they will likely turn brown or curl. If you suspect that high humidity levels are responsible for your indoor plants’ lackluster appearance, look into investing in an affordable dehumidifier. What’s your budget?

It can be very tempting to run out and buy a dozen new plants without thinking about whether you have enough space for them. But before you start your shopping spree, think carefully about your budget. You may want a collection of rare and exotic orchids, but if you can’t afford them, stick with something less expensive—or focus on just one type of plant that fits your room’s decor. And if you’ve never grown indoor plants before, it’s also a good idea to talk with an expert at your local nursery: They’ll be able to tell you which options will fit well in your space and give them a chance to help troubleshoot any issues that might crop up along the way.

Can you commit time to caring for indoor plants?

Before you buy a plant for your office or home, make sure you’re going to be able to commit time and attention to care for it. While most indoor plants are easy-to-care-for and don’t require much from their owner, they still need basic things like water and sunlight. You should know ahead of time if these needs match your schedule or habits. If not, there are other low-maintenance options available that will still help create a natural atmosphere in your space—like air plants, moss balls, and succulents.

Are you comfortable with pests?

Pests are a major problem for indoor plants, but your ability to deal with these intruders will help determine how effective you are in taking care of your plants. If you enjoy dealing with pests, then it shouldn’t be much of an issue when working with indoor plants. However, if these little creatures make you squeamish, then there’s a good chance that you aren’t comfortable working with pests.

Do you have pets/children in the house who might knock over or uproot indoor plants by accident?

So, you’ve decided that you want to get some indoor plants because they look nice or help with your allergies. But once they start growing bigger, do you have pets or children in your house who might knock over or uproot indoor plants by accident? There are many beautiful varieties of indoor plants available for sale in stores and online, but it’s important to ensure that a plant is robust enough for where you’re going to put it. Ask questions about whether plants are toxic if ingested; very young children can accidentally ingest smaller, leafy varieties. Also, ask whether a plant will grow tall enough (or too large) for your space and make sure you know how quickly each variety grows so that you don’t end up over watering or under watering them!
